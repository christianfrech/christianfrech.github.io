    • Multiscale simulations of the lithium metal anode microstructure in solid state batteries

    Diagram of all possible vacancy migrations acocunted for in kMC simulation

    Dendrite growth plagues lithium metal batteries, leading to shortage & cell failure when cycling at sufficiently high current density. The growth of voids is linked to the nucleation and growth of dendrites via current concentration. However, these processes often originate on nanometer length scales and are obscured from view, or the features are disturbed or destroyed during measurement via spectroscopy techniques. We have developed a multiscale framework to observe the nucleation and dissolution of voids, beginning with DFT calculations to observe the bidining energies of vacancies to vacancy clusters and solid electrolyte interfaces. These results were used in kMC simulations to visualize and qualtify the dissolution of vacancies in the lithium metal and at the solid electrolyte interface. As a function of void energy below bulk and solid electrolyte binding energy, maps to inform experiementalists of the fundamental properties of lithium were developed, along with visualizations.
